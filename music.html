    • Music

    I think my life would not be worth it, if there wasn't any music. I have changing tastes, but there are constants. Classial music is really my background. My grandpa founded and conducted an orchestra so I spent many difficult but happy hours attending his concerts from a very very early age. But in contrast, I love to rock! All its varieties have been a huge part of my life. More recently I have got into alternative music which tends to be more emotionally engaging. All music that is profound to me, I assign to represent milestones. It's a good way to keep track of the mayhem that occurs in one's life.

    I have always been very musical. I started at the age of three playing the violin, which didn't last very long. Then the typical primary school child playing the recorder! For about 4 or 5 years I played the cello, which was great, but the practicing was a chore. But my favourites will always be the guitar and singing. Since 5, I have played the guitar, and singing has always gone well with it. They are my solid go to for whatever mood I am in and wherever I am I sing to myself. Each guitar I have played has meant a lot to me. They make for pretty loyal and understanding companions!

    In the past I have been in a couple of bands. Sometimes I was frontman, in others I was just a rhythm guitarist But none of them really worked out. I put it down to creative differences. It has been quite a few years since I have been in a band and I would very much like to get back in to one. I will see if I can set up a band or, failing that, join one. Even if that doesn't work out i'll continue to play. What would be useful in the near future is to put in some more hours dedicated to practicing and improving my guitar skills. Then I might even be able to be the lead guitarist! Ooh I might also learn the piano too.

    Bands

    Brian Jonestown Massacre

    They are, quite frankly, an extremely underrated and underappreciated band. Predominantly known as a psychedelic rock band, they are credited with reviving the sound of original 60's rock and pop and blending it for the modern times.




    Red Hot Chili Peppers

    Initially a formidable funk group starting way back in 1983, over the years the Red Hot Chili Peppers have morphed into one of the most popular rock bands in the world. What's more, they continue to release excellent music which is a rare thing indeed with an aging band.

    Nirvana

    A legendary and tragic story, Nirvana's life was epic and wild but far, far too short. In the space of 7 years, the band released three albums, all of which are highly rated and continue to sell to this day.

    Radiohead

    One of the most versatile bands of the modern era. What is also impressive, are the consistently high quality albums they have released over their long career and also that they have never once changed their line-up.

    Guitarists

    Jimi Hendrix

    Regarded by most as the greatest rock guitarist to ever live, Jimi Hendrix is known for three things: Being left handed, dying at 27 and absolutely shredding on the guitar.

    Jimmy Page

    With his iconic double necked guitar pictured above, Jimmy Page rocked with many great bands. He's most famous for being the guitarist for the Yardbirds and then becoming the legendary guitarist for Led Zeppelin.

    David Gilmour

    His beautiful solos will make you feel all sorts of emotions and, along with his voice, are credited with giving Pink Floyd their notable soft, pure and crazy sound.

    Keith Richards

    Many believe this man will never die, and i'm inclined to believe them. Like all the greats, Richards is a self-taught guitarist and the lead for the Rolling Stones. His long career is legendary and spectacular, even the ugly bits!
