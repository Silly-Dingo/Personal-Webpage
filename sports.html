    Boxing and UFC

    Jack Dempsey

    The Manassa Mauler, Jack Dempsey competed for 13 years and 7 of them as the reigning heavyweight champion of the world. Known for his exceptionally aggressive strategy and incredible punching power, he is regarded as one of the greatest boxers in the history of the sport.

    James J. Braddock

    Otherwise known as the Cinderella Man, James J. Braddock is perhaps most famous for his incredible comeback to become heavyweight champion of the world after living in poverty during the Great Depression. His determination and drive to provide for his family is inspirational to many.

    Conor McGregor

    You either love him, or hate him. Conor McGregor is without question a controversial figure in UFC and sport in general. However, aside from his persona outside the ring, he is an excellent fighter who trains hard and, more often than not, backs up what he says with action.

    Jon Jones

    The UFC's #1 pound-for-pound fighter, Jon Jones is widely regarded as one of the greatest in the history of mixed martial arts. He holds many records including the most title defences, most wins, longest win streak and most submission victories. He is quite unstoppable.

    Cricket

    Andrew Flintoff

    Freddie Flintoff is still considered to be one of the best all-rounders of modern cricket. His fast paced bowling style and aggressive batting made Flintoff a key player for England after his test debut in 1998 and saw him become 'Man of the Series' in the legendary 2005 Ashes Test.

    Shane Warne

    Shane Warne is in the history books as one of the greatest spin bowlers in cricket. Stacking up over 700 wickets during his test career, he is best known for bowling the 'ball of the century'. If you haven't seen it, type it in on YouTube. It's considered one of the greatest wickets in history.

    India National Cricket Team

    Despite what you might think, India's national team was not always that good at cricket. Until the 1970s, they were considered one of the weaker teams in Test cricket. However, since then, it has produced some of the greatest cricketers in history. With batsmen like Sachin Tendulker and spinners like Anil Kumble.

    England National Cricket Team

    Being the inventors of a game doesn't mean you'll always be the best and England has certainly had difficulty with form, losing almost as much as they win. But over the years, they have produced some spectacular cricketing performances and continue to churn out consistently excellent cricketers.

    Rugby

    Sebastien Chabal

    Nicknamed the Caveman, Sebastien Chabal was known for his long hair, big beard and ferocious tackling. During his long career playing at both club and international level, Chabal was utterly feared on the pitch, with opponents often struggling to deal with his aggression.

    Jason Robinson

    Jason Robinson is widely regarded as one of England's best wingers and fullbacks. Playing 51 times for England he was known for his effective side stepping, his blistering speed and thus his ability to beat defenders. Because of these skills he racked up an impressive number of tries.

    New Zealand National Rugby Union Team

    More commonly known as the All Blacks, this team has more wins than losses against every opponent they've faced. They have a 77% winning record in test-match rugby, the highest for an international team. Furthermore, they have produced some of the greatest players ever.

    England National Rugby Union Team

    What's not to like about the England Rugby team? Despite losing their first ever match way back in 1871, England has participated in some of the most exciting matches in the sport's history. Currently they are the only team from the Northern Hemisphere to win the Rugby World Cup!
